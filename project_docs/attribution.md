# Attribution and Source Basis

This project is an LPG and wiki prototype based on a single primary source document:

Segal, A. A., & Greenspan, I. (2024). The Americanization of Israeli Conservative Civil Society: A critical community and transnational transference perspective. *Israel Studies, 29*(2), 144–169.

Segal and Greenspan’s article is the source of the scholarly argument, empirical discussion, and source material modeled in this prototype. This project does not claim authorship of the article’s research, argument, or findings. The article is credited as the primary source throughout the demo and documentation.

Jacob Epstein’s contribution is the transformation of the article into an article-to-LPG and wiki prototype. This includes the modeling of entities and relationships, the construction of the interactive LPG, the design of the wiki layer, the creation of internal wiki links, and the editorial process used to iterate on and finalize the digital cards that serve as wiki pages.

The project was developed through an AI-with-human-in-the-loop workflow (Mosqueira-Rey et al., 2023). In this workflow, AI tools proposed candidate nodes, edges, metadata tags, and wiki prose, while Jacob Epstein reviewed, corrected, rejected, revised, and approved modeling decisions. This was not a fully automated extraction project. Human review was central to the process, especially for temporal relationships, ambiguous entity categories, prose quality, terminology, and the distinction between graph nodes and metadata tags.

The wiki layer is also a project contribution. Wiki pages were generated through an AI-with-human-in-the-loop workflow (Mosqueira-Rey et al., 2023), based on the graph structure and especially on node and edge relationships. They were then revised into narrative prose so that readers could move between the graph and explanatory text. Internal wiki links were added to support navigation between the digital cards of related nodes and edges in a style inspired by the familiar Wikipedia design.
